It's all baby carrots' fault.

Or actually it's the fault of Americans who didn't buy enough carrots and the industry had to find a new, appealing way to sell them. So, they chopped the carrots, and people loved them.

Because we just love snacks. Over half of all Americans’ eating is already snacking, according to a 2016 Hartman Group’s industry study called “The Future of Snacking.”

Healthy snacks, like chopped vegetables, is one of the biggest trends in the food industry right now, but to be honest, every time I see them, I think it’s just a waste of money – I could chop the veggies and fruits myself. But how much more am I actually paying, and how long it would take me to chop them myself? I did a test. See the results by clicking the items below.

These six items above you find most often ready-chopped in the supermarkets. But what if you want something else, something that you really hate to chop? The newest Whole Foods in New York, has the solution: a "Produce Butcher" who will chop, dice, julienne and mince whatever vegetables customers wants prepared. The company charges $1 per pound, or per individually priced item.
The veggie butcher at Whole Foods, Bryant Park. April 1, 2017. (Photo: Pauliina Siniauer)
So which fruits and vegetables are worth paying extra? The lightest vegetables and fruits take most time per pound to chop, but the heaviest ones tend to cost the most. It all comes down to how much ready-chopped fruits or vegetables you want – and which ones you hate to chop.
