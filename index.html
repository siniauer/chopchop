It's all baby carrots fault.

Or actually it's the fault of Americans who didn't buy enough carrots and the industry had to find a new, appealing way to sell carrots. So they chopped them – and people loved them. No peeling, no mess. A colorful and crunchy piece to pop into your mouth that is so sweet you almost forget it’s healthy – a perfect snack.

And we just love snacks. According to a recent study from the Hartman Group: "with 91% of consumers snacking multiple times throughout the day, snacking is essential to daily eating for most Americans now and accounts for 50% of all eatings."

The biggest trends in the industry are fresh and healthy snacks – like chopped vegetables. But I’ll be honest with you, every time I see those in the supermarket, I think that it’s just a waste of money, I could chop the veggies and fruits myself. But how much more am I actually paying and how much longer it would take me to do that? I did a test. The results you can find below.

These six items above you most often can find ready-chopped. But what if you want something else, something that you really hate to chop? The newest Whole Foods in New York City, has the solution: a "Produce Butcher" who will chop, dice, and mince whatever vegetables customers wants prepared. The company charges $1 per pound, or per individually priced item.
The veggie butcher at Whole Foods, Bryant Park. April 1, 2017. (Photo: Pauliina Siniauer)
So which fruits and vegetables are worth paying extra? The lightest vegetables and fruits take most time per pound, but the heaviest ones tend to cost most. It all comes down to how much ready-chopped fruits or vegetables do you want – and which ones you hate to chop.
